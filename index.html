Psychological Experiences of Law Enforcement Officers Responding to Critical Incidents Alone and With a Partner

Forensic Psychology, PhD Dissertation

This study examined the psychological impact of critical incident response among officers operating alone versus with a partner. Interviews with nine participants highlighted trauma exposure, safety perception, and peer support strategies.

View Full Abstract

Using a qualitative design, the dissertation examined how context, team structure, and support systems shape officer resilience after high-stress events. Mixed Methods Healthcare Kenya

Effectiveness of Radiation Protection in Detection of Exposures among Healthcare Workers in Level 4 and 5 Hospitals in Nyeri County, Kenya

Healthcare / Radiation Safety

This project assessed radiation exposure levels, staff awareness, and protective practice in Kenyan hospitals. Findings showed exposure remained relatively low, but uneven training and monitoring highlighted clear areas for safety improvement.

View Full Abstract

The research combined survey data, compliance observations, and stakeholder interviews to evaluate real-world implementation of radiation safety standards. While most facilities met baseline protection levels, results showed inconsistency in staff training cycles and monitoring practices, supporting targeted capacity-building and stronger institutional oversight.

Human Resource Management / Security Studies

This dissertation tested how ergonomics, supervisor support, culture, and reward systems influence employee performance. Results showed strong predictive effects and supported structured policy improvements for private security firms.

This dissertation used a structured analytical framework to test how work conditions influence productivity and retention in security firms.
